ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This Supplement clarifies the conceptual, observational, and logical status of
vacuum energy and the cosmological constant within the framework of the
0-Sphere model. The aim is not to derive the observed value of the cosmological
constant (Lambda_obs), nor to propose a dynamical mechanism for its smallness.
Instead, this note establishes a strict separation between two frequently
conflated questions: (i) why the vacuum energy problem arises within local
quantum field theory and whether it constitutes a physical inconsistency, and
(ii) what the empirical origin of the observed cosmological constant may be.

The 0-Sphere model addresses only the first question. By restricting physical
observables to closed line-integral processes associated with internal
circulation, the premises leading to the conventional vacuum energy divergence
are shown to be invalid. The second question remains open and is explicitly not
resolved in the present framework. No claim is made that the 0-Sphere model
predicts or explains the numerical value of Lambda_obs.

Key topics include:
- Vacuum energy as a category error in local QFT (operator-ordering artifacts
  misidentified as gravitational sources)
- Localization of energy in the 0-Sphere model: internal circulation vs.
  ubiquitous vacuum energy density
- Logical decoupling of the absence of vacuum energy from the existence of an
  effective cosmological constant
- Observational status and falsifiability criteria
- Scope and future directions: topological invariants, boundary conditions,
  phase-frozen configurations

================================================================================
KEY RESULTS
================================================================================

What the model CAN address:
- Demonstrating that the premises of vacuum energy divergence are invalid
  within integral-based ontology
- Identifying step 5 of the standard QFT derivation (gravitational coupling
  of operator-ordering artifacts) as a category error
- Clarifying the logical separation between the vacuum energy problem and
  the cosmological constant problem
- Providing falsifiability criteria for the integral-based restriction

What the model CANNOT currently derive:
- The numerical value of Lambda_obs
- The dynamical mechanism responsible for the smallness of Lambda_obs
- The specific origin among the speculative possibilities (boundary
  conditions, topological invariants, phase-frozen configurations, etc.)

The cosmological constant problem is not solved but rendered inapplicable
within this framework. Lambda_obs remains an empirical input awaiting a
deeper theoretical interpretation.
