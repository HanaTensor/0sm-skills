ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This supplementary document serves as a methodological manifesto for the 
0-Sphere model, clarifying the distinction between perturbative and 
non-perturbative approaches to theoretical physics.

The document addresses why the electron anomalous magnetic moment can be 
understood geometrically through first-principles reasoning within the 
0-Sphere framework, while the fine-structure constant alpha remains outside 
the current scope. This distinction reflects a fundamental methodological 
difference: conventional quantum electrodynamics treats alpha as a perturbative 
expansion parameter, whereas the 0-Sphere model adopts a deterministic, 
background-independent approach grounded in geometric realism.

Key topics include:
- Non-perturbative derivation of Zitterbewegung velocity v_ZB ~ 0.04047c
- Geometric origin of gamma = 1 + a from Lorentz contraction
- Reinterpretation of Thomas precession and spin ontology
- Closed geometric equations without free parameters
- Philosophical foundations: realism vs. instrumentalism
- Explicit scope delimitation and theoretical transparency

KEY RESULTS
================================================================================

What the model CAN derive:
- Zitterbewegung RMS velocity: v_ZB^(RMS) ~ 0.04047c (~ 12,100 km/s)
- Peak velocity: v_ZB^(max) ~ 0.048c (~ 14,400 km/s)
- Geometric origin of electron anomalous magnetic moment g-2
- Spin-1/2 quantization from doubled angular frequency
- Energy conservation structure from Stefan-Boltzmann law

What the model CANNOT currently derive:
- Fine-structure constant alpha ~ 1/137
- Electron rest mass m_e
- Lepton mass hierarchy
- Strong and weak interactions

This explicit delimitation distinguishes established deductive results from 
speculative extensions, ensuring scientific integrity.
