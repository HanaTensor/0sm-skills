\title{One Internal Oscillator: The Symplectic Origin of the Complex Structure, Equivalence to the Dirac Rest Solutions, and the Geometric Origin of the Quartic Energy in the 0-Sphere Model}

\author{Satoshi Hanamura}
\email{hana.tensor@gmail.com}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
The 0-Sphere model describes the electron as two black-body kernels and a captured photon, and organizes their energetics around a single trigonometric identity, $E_0=E_0[\cos^4(\omega t/2)+\sin^4(\omega t/2)+\tfrac12\sin^2\omega t]$.
Two features of this identity have remained unexplained: its relation to the Dirac equation, and the appearance of \emph{fourth} powers of the internal amplitude where relativistic quantum mechanics expects \emph{quadratic} (bilinear) energy densities.
This paper resolves both, and in doing so ties four previously separate results to one source.
First, the amplitude implied by the identity---the half-angle rotor $\chi=(\cos\tfrac{\omega t}{2},\sin\tfrac{\omega t}{2})^{\!\top}$---is shown to satisfy a first-order spinor equation $i\partial_t\chi=\tfrac{\omega}{2}\sigma_y\chi$ whose generator squares to $(\omega/2)^2\openone$, the rest-frame Dirac square-root of Klein--Gordon; under $\hbar\omega/2=mc^2$ the rotor is proved unitarily equivalent to an equal superposition of the Dirac positive- and negative-energy rest solutions.
Second, the complex unit entering this equivalence is not imported but generated internally: it is the symplectic complex structure of the model's simple harmonic oscillation, and the oscillator's velocity--acceleration pair coincides component-wise with the Dirac zitterbewegung coherences.
Third---the central new result---the fourth-power (Stefan--Boltzmann-looking) energy channels are shown to be exactly quadratic forms on the Bloch sphere of the two-level system, $T_{e1}=\tfrac14 E_0(1+s_z)^2$, $T_{e2}=\tfrac14 E_0(1-s_z)^2$, $\gamma^*_{K\!E}=\tfrac12 E_0 s_x^2$, and to be \emph{derivable} from the model's radiation-gradient relation, the oscillator kinetic energy, and energy conservation---without an independent Stefan--Boltzmann postulate.
The single variable change connecting the quartic and quadratic descriptions is the Hopf (double-cover) map $\chi\mapsto\Bloch=\chi^\dagger\bm{\sigma}\chi$.
We argue this settles a foundational question of the model---whether the electron's fermionic fourth power is thermodynamic or geometric in origin---in favour of a geometric origin, with the thermodynamic $T^4$ reading a dual re-description of the same quadratic Bloch structure rather than a second, independent origin.
Relative to the companion construction that builds spin two-valuedness from the square root of the hyperspherical Laplacian on $S^3$~\cite{ref:p64}, these results supply the complex-structure, Bloch-vector, and quartic-derivation content that its operator-theoretic treatment omits, and answer in the affirmative its stated open question of whether the energy identity's square-root signature is more than formal.
We state the limitations without softening them, and confirm every analytic claim numerically.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

\subsection{The model and its central identity}

The 0-Sphere model represents the electron not as a point but as a small internal system: two perfect black-body ``kernels'' (bare electrons, treated as spinors) together with a captured photon whose exchange between the kernels binds the structure~\cite{ref:p1}.
The bookkeeping of the internal energy is carried by one algebraic statement, cited by every subsequent paper of the series,
\begin{equation}
E_0 = E_0\!\left[\cos^4\!\tfrac{\omega t}{2} + \sin^4\!\tfrac{\omega t}{2} + \tfrac12\sin^2\!\omega t\right],
\label{eq:central}
\end{equation}
in which the three bracketed terms are read, respectively, as the thermal potential energies of the two kernels and the kinetic energy of the captured (``virtual'') photon,
\begin{equation}
\frac{T_{e1}}{E_0}=\cos^4\!\tfrac{\omega t}{2},\quad
\frac{T_{e2}}{E_0}=\sin^4\!\tfrac{\omega t}{2},\quad
\frac{\gamma^*_{K\!E}}{E_0}=\tfrac12\sin^2\!\omega t .
\label{eq:channels}
\end{equation}
Because the bracket equals unity for every $\omega$ and every $t$, the total $E_0$ is constant; it is this constancy that the model calls energy conservation.
The quartic powers are motivated in Ref.~\cite{ref:p1} by analogy with the Stefan--Boltzmann law, under which the energy radiated by a black body scales as the fourth power of temperature; if the ``temperature'' of a kernel is proportional to $\cos(\omega t/2)$ or $\sin(\omega t/2)$, the corresponding energy scales as the fourth power.

\subsection{Two puzzles, and why they matter}

Equation~\eqref{eq:central} is, as an equation, a trigonometric identity: it is true by algebra, independent of any dynamics.
Its physical content therefore cannot lie in the mere fact that three non-negative terms sum to unity---that is guaranteed---but in the structure it encodes.
Two features of that structure have never been made explicit, and each is a genuine puzzle for anyone reading the model against standard physics.

\paragraph{Puzzle 1: the half-angle.}
The energies~\eqref{eq:channels} are built from the \emph{half}-angle $\omega t/2$, whereas the cross term carries the \emph{full} angle $\omega t$.
A dependence on $\omega t/2$ is the hallmark of a $720^\circ$ (double-cover) periodicity, i.e.\ of a spinor.
This raises an unavoidable question: what is the relation between Eq.~\eqref{eq:central} and the Dirac equation, the canonical description of a relativistic spin-$\tfrac12$ particle?
Earlier work took steps---the two kernels were identified with Dirac's positive- and negative-energy solutions~\cite{ref:p7}, the internal motion was modeled as a harmonic oscillation with a sub-luminal zitterbewegung prediction~\cite{ref:p10}, and the spinorial $\theta/2$ character of the quartic terms was named as an open question~\cite{ref:p43}---but no explicit equation of motion for the amplitude underlying Eq.~\eqref{eq:central} was ever written, and ``identification'' was never upgraded to a proof of equivalence.

\paragraph{Puzzle 2: the fourth power.}
In relativistic quantum mechanics the energy density of a Dirac field is \emph{bilinear}---quadratic in the amplitude $\psi$, of the form $\psi^\dagger H\psi$.
The 0-Sphere energies~\eqref{eq:channels}, by contrast, are \emph{quartic} in the internal amplitude.
This is not a cosmetic difference.
It forces a foundational question about the model's very identity: is the electron's fermionic fourth power a genuine \emph{thermodynamic} law (Stefan--Boltzmann $T^4$), so that the model's black-body language is fundamental and irreducible; or is it a \emph{geometric} artifact of writing energies in the spinor (half-angle) variable, so that the fourth power is a standard quadratic energy seen through the double cover?
The answer decides whether the 0-Sphere model \emph{contradicts} the bilinear structure of Dirac theory or \emph{embeds} it, and whether ``thermodynamics'' is a primitive of the model or a re-description of spinor geometry.
We will refer to this as the \emph{root question}.

\subsection{What this paper does}

We resolve both puzzles and connect them.
Sections~\ref{sec:rotor}--\ref{sec:symplectic} treat Puzzle~1: we write the first-order spinor equation the amplitude obeys (Sec.~\ref{sec:rotor}), prove its unitary equivalence to the Dirac rest solutions (Sec.~\ref{sec:equiv}), and show that the complex unit needed for that equivalence is generated by the model's own harmonic oscillation rather than borrowed from Dirac theory (Sec.~\ref{sec:symplectic}).
Section~\ref{sec:quartic} treats Puzzle~2 and is the central new contribution: introducing the Bloch (Stokes) vector of the two-level system as the natural full-angle observable, we show that the quartic energy channels are exactly quadratic forms on the Bloch sphere, that they follow from conservation together with the model's own radiation-gradient and oscillator relations, and that the single variable change relating the quartic and quadratic descriptions is the Hopf double-cover map.
We then argue that this settles the root question in favour of a geometric origin, with thermodynamics a dual reading.
Section~\ref{sec:ledger} organizes the three internal frequencies, Sec.~\ref{sec:limits} states the limitations, and Sec.~\ref{sec:conclusion} concludes.
Appendix~\ref{app:numeric} reproduces the numerical verification; Appendix~\ref{app:bloch} gives the explicit Bloch-map algebra and the derivation of the quartic; Appendix~\ref{app:boson} works out the bosonic contrast.
The presentation is deliberately self-contained and step-by-step, aimed at a reader familiar with the Dirac equation and elementary two-level (Bloch-sphere) quantum mechanics but not with the 0-Sphere series.

\subsection{Relation to the hyperspherical square-root construction}
\label{sec:rel64}

A companion paper in the series~\cite{ref:p64} constructs the electron's spin two-valuedness by taking the square root of the scalar Laplacian on the spatial three-sphere $S^3\cong SU(2)$.
There the Dirac operator $\mathcal{D}_{S^3}$ obeys the Lichnerowicz identity $\mathcal{D}_{S^3}^2=-\Delta_{S^3}+R/4$ (with scalar curvature $R=6$ on the unit sphere), its eigenvalues form signed half-integer pairs $\pm(\ell+\tfrac32)$, and the obstruction to choosing one sign globally is exactly the $SU(2)\to SO(3)$ double cover; this yields the $720^\circ$ periodicity and the sign change of $\cos(\theta/2)$ under $\theta\to\theta+2\pi$.
That treatment is operator-theoretic and Euclidean.
By its own account it does not employ complex numbers, Bloch or Stokes vectors, or a first-principles derivation of the quartic energy; it leaves open (its Appendix~C) whether the energy identity's square-root signature is ``more than formal,'' and it defers the Wick-rotation bridge from the Euclidean $S^3$ construction to Minkowski dynamics.
The present paper is the dynamical, Lorentzian-time counterpart to that construction.
Our square root appears not as a spatial differential operator but as the first-order temporal evolution of the internal clock, Eq.~\eqref{eq:foe}; we supply precisely the complex-structure, Bloch-vector, and quartic-derivation content that Ref.~\cite{ref:p64} omits, and we answer its Appendix~C question in the affirmative (Sec.~\ref{sec:root}).
We do not claim to construct the Wick bridge itself; rather, the two papers realize the same double cover at the two ends a full bridge would connect---a Euclidean spatial operator on one side, Lorentzian temporal dynamics on the other.

\section{The kernel rotor and its equation of motion}
\label{sec:rotor}

\subsection{The amplitude behind the identity}

The energies~\eqref{eq:channels} are fourth powers of the two half-angle functions $\cos(\omega t/2)$ and $\sin(\omega t/2)$.
It is natural to collect these two functions into a single two-component real object, the \emph{kernel rotor},
\begin{equation}
\chi(t) \equiv \begin{pmatrix}\cos\tfrac{\omega t}{2}\\[3pt] \sin\tfrac{\omega t}{2}\end{pmatrix},
\qquad \chi^\dagger\chi = 1 ,
\label{eq:rotor}
\end{equation}
which is normalized for all $t$.
Its squared components are the (normalized) \emph{occupations} of the two kernels,
\begin{equation}
n_1 \equiv \abs{\chi_1}^2=\cos^2\!\tfrac{\omega t}{2},\qquad
n_2 \equiv \abs{\chi_2}^2=\sin^2\!\tfrac{\omega t}{2},
\label{eq:occ}
\end{equation}
with $n_1+n_2=1$, and the thermal energies are the \emph{squares} of the occupations, $T_{e1}=E_0 n_1^2$ and $T_{e2}=E_0 n_2^2$.
The rotor is the object the identity is ``about'': the energies are functions of $\chi$, and the double-cover periodicity---$\chi(t+2\pi/\omega)=-\chi(t)$, so that a full $720^\circ$ ($\omega t=4\pi$) is required to return $\chi$ to itself---resides in it.

\subsection{A first-order spinor equation}

Differentiate Eq.~\eqref{eq:rotor} once:
\begin{equation}
\dot\chi
=\frac{\omega}{2}\begin{pmatrix}-\sin\tfrac{\omega t}{2}\\[3pt]\ \cos\tfrac{\omega t}{2}\end{pmatrix}
=\frac{\omega}{2}\begin{pmatrix}0&-1\\ 1&0\end{pmatrix}\chi .
\label{eq:deriv}
\end{equation}
The real antisymmetric generator can be written in terms of the Pauli matrix $\sigma_y=\left(\begin{smallmatrix}0&-i\\ i&0\end{smallmatrix}\right)$ as $\left(\begin{smallmatrix}0&-1\\ 1&0\end{smallmatrix}\right)=-i\sigma_y$.
Equation~\eqref{eq:deriv} is therefore exactly a first-order equation of Schr\"odinger/Dirac type,
\begin{equation}
i\,\partial_t\chi = H\chi, \qquad H=\frac{\omega}{2}\,\sigma_y ,
\label{eq:foe}
\end{equation}
in units $\hbar=1$ (we restore $\hbar$ where physical energies appear).
The generator $H$ is Hermitian, with eigenvalues $\pm\omega/2$, and---because any traceless $2\times2$ Hermitian matrix squares to a multiple of the identity---it satisfies
\begin{equation}
H^2=\left(\frac{\omega}{2}\right)^{\!2}\openone
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
(i\,\partial_t)^2\chi=\left(\frac{\omega}{2}\right)^{\!2}\chi .
\label{eq:kgroot}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Why this is the Dirac structure.}
The free Dirac equation at rest ($\mathbf{p}=0$) reads $i\hbar\partial_t\psi=\beta mc^2\psi$ with $\beta^2=\openone$, so that $(i\hbar\partial_t)^2\psi=(mc^2)^2\psi$: the first-order Dirac operator is a \emph{square root} of the second-order Klein--Gordon operator.
Equation~\eqref{eq:kgroot} is precisely this square-root relation for a particle of rest energy $\hbar\omega/2$.
Identifying the positive eigenvalue with the rest energy,
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;\frac{\hbar\omega}{2}=mc^2\;}
\label{eq:idmass}
\end{equation}
makes the eigenvalues of $H$ equal to $\pm mc^2$, reproducing the standard positive/negative Dirac rest energies $E\psi^\pm=\pm mc^2\psi^\pm$ that Ref.~\cite{ref:p7} identified with the two kernels.
No new postulate has been introduced between Eqs.~\eqref{eq:rotor} and~\eqref{eq:idmass}: writing the identity's amplitude as a rotor and differentiating once already produces the rest-frame Dirac structure.
The square-root relation~\eqref{eq:kgroot} is the temporal, two-level realization of the Dirac factorization of Klein--Gordon that Ref.~\cite{ref:p64} develops spatially through the hyperspherical Laplacian on $S^3$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:rel64}); here it is a first-order evolution in time rather than an elliptic operator on the sphere.

\section{Unitary equivalence to the Dirac rest solutions}
\label{sec:equiv}

The rotor~\eqref{eq:rotor} is real, and its component occupations~\eqref{eq:occ} oscillate; the Dirac rest solutions $e^{\mp imc^2t/\hbar}$ are complex, with fixed occupations.
That these describe the \emph{same} two-level system is not obvious, and we establish it by transforming to the energy eigenbasis of $H$.

The eigenvectors of $H=\tfrac{\omega}{2}\sigma_y$ are
\begin{align}
u_{+}&=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}1\\ i\end{pmatrix}\quad (E_{+}=+\tfrac{\omega}{2}),
\notag\\
u_{-}&=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}1\\ -i\end{pmatrix}\quad (E_{-}=-\tfrac{\omega}{2}).
\label{eq:eigvec}
\end{align}
Projecting the rotor onto this basis, $c_\pm=u_\pm^\dagger\chi(0)=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt2}$ at $t=0$, and evolving each eigenmode by its phase $e^{-iE_\pm t}$, gives the exact decomposition
\begin{equation}
\chi(t)=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\Big[\,e^{-i\omega t/2}\,u_{+}\;+\;e^{+i\omega t/2}\,u_{-}\,\Big].
\label{eq:decomp}
\end{equation}
One verifies Eq.~\eqref{eq:decomp} by expansion: the upper component is $\tfrac12(e^{-i\omega t/2}+e^{+i\omega t/2})=\cos\tfrac{\omega t}{2}$, and the lower is $\tfrac{i}{2}(e^{-i\omega t/2}-e^{+i\omega t/2})=\sin\tfrac{\omega t}{2}$, recovering Eq.~\eqref{eq:rotor}.
The matrix $U=[\,u_+\ u_-\,]^\dagger$ is a fixed (time-independent) unitary, so Eq.~\eqref{eq:decomp} is a genuine change of basis, not an approximation or a limit.
Under Eq.~\eqref{eq:idmass} the two coefficients are $e^{\mp i\omega t/2}=e^{\mp imc^2t/\hbar}$: the positive- and negative-energy Dirac rest solutions $\psi^+$ and $\psi^-$.
We may therefore state:

\begin{theorem}[Rest-frame equivalence]
\label{thm:equiv}
The 0-Sphere kernel rotor $\chi(t)$ of Eq.~\eqref{eq:rotor} is unitarily equivalent, via the fixed unitary $U$ diagonalizing $H=\tfrac{\omega}{2}\sigma_y$, to the equal-weight superposition $\tfrac{1}{\sqrt2}(\psi^{+}+\psi^{-})$ of the Dirac positive- and negative-energy rest solutions, provided $\hbar\omega/2=mc^2$.
The kernel (occupation) basis and the Dirac energy basis are one Hilbert space in two frames related by $U$.
\end{theorem}

Three physical consequences follow immediately.
(i) The two branch weights are equal and constant, $\abs{c_\pm}^2=\tfrac12$; what oscillates in the kernel frame---the occupations, hence the thermal energies---is pure relative phase in the energy frame.
(ii) The mean Hamiltonian vanishes, $\expval{H}{\chi}=\tfrac12E_++\tfrac12E_-=0$, so the symmetric two-kernel state is \emph{not} a stationary free electron (a pure positive-energy branch, $c_-=0$, has $\expval{H}=+mc^2$) but the balanced $\pm$ superposition.
(iii) An equal superposition of $\pm$ energy branches is exactly the condition under which zitterbewegung appears in standard Dirac theory, since the trembling arises from the interference of the two branches~\cite{ref:schrodinger,ref:thaller}.
The 0-Sphere electron is thus, in this reduction, the maximally-zitterbewegung state, so the model's insistence on a permanently oscillating internal structure and the standard condition for zitterbewegung coincide rather than conflict.

\section{The complex unit is generated internally}
\label{sec:symplectic}

Theorem~\ref{thm:equiv} required promoting the real rotor to a complex superposition; the imaginary unit $i$ entered through the eigenvectors~\eqref{eq:eigvec}.
Had that $i$ to be borrowed from Dirac theory, the equivalence would be a re-basing of standard quantum mechanics rather than a statement internal to the model.
We show the $i$ is supplied by the model's own dynamics.

\subsection{The internal oscillation is a phase-space rotation}

The 0-Sphere model drives the captured photon as a simple harmonic oscillator by the radiation gradient $\partial_\theta(T_{e2}-T_{e1})=E_0\sin\theta$ (with $\theta=\omega t$), and Ref.~\cite{ref:p10} writes its internal velocity and acceleration as
\begin{equation}
v_{\gamma^*}=\cos\omega t,\qquad a_{\gamma^*}=-\sin\omega t .
\label{eq:va}
\end{equation}
These are not two independent sinusoids but the two projections of a single uniform rotation.
For any harmonic oscillator $\ddot x=-\omega^2 x$, define the complex phase-space amplitude
\begin{equation}
\alpha=\sqrt{\tfrac{m\omega}{2\hbar}}\;x+\frac{i}{\sqrt{2m\hbar\omega}}\;p ,
\label{eq:alphadef}
\end{equation}
which combines position and momentum into one complex number.
Its equation of motion is first-order and diagonal,
\begin{equation}
\dot\alpha=-i\omega\,\alpha
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\alpha(t)=\alpha(0)\,e^{-i\omega t},\quad \abs{\alpha}=\text{const}.
\label{eq:alpha}
\end{equation}
The multiplication by $i$ in Eq.~\eqref{eq:alpha} is the \emph{symplectic complex structure} $J$ on the $(x,p)$ plane: the unique (up to orientation) operator with $J^2=-1$ that rotates phase space along the canonical flow.
It is intrinsic to the oscillator, not adjoined to it.
A sinusoid, in this language, is the shadow of a rotation, and the rotation \emph{is} the complex structure: the same object that Eq.~\eqref{eq:decomp} needed.

\subsection{The oscillator components are the Dirac coherences}

The correspondence is not merely one of shared frequency; the components match exactly.
Evaluating the Pauli coherences of the two-level system in the rotor state~\eqref{eq:rotor},
\begin{align}
\expval{\sigma_z}&=\abs{\chi_1}^2-\abs{\chi_2}^2=\cos^2\!\tfrac{\omega t}{2}-\sin^2\!\tfrac{\omega t}{2}=\cos\omega t,
\notag\\
\expval{\sigma_x}&=2\,\mathrm{Re}(\chi_1^*\chi_2)=2\cos\tfrac{\omega t}{2}\sin\tfrac{\omega t}{2}=\sin\omega t,
\label{eq:pauli}
\end{align}
so that, comparing with Eq.~\eqref{eq:va},
\begin{equation}
(v_{\gamma^*},\,a_{\gamma^*})=(\expval{\sigma_z},\,-\expval{\sigma_x}).
\label{eq:coh}
\end{equation}
The internal oscillator's velocity--acceleration vector traces the same circle, at the same rate $\omega$, as the Dirac zitterbewegung coherence vector.
Combining Eqs.~\eqref{eq:alpha}--\eqref{eq:coh}: the ``sine wave'' of the radiation gradient $E_0\sin\omega t$ and the ``sine wave'' of Dirac zitterbewegung are projections of one phase-space rotation, and therefore share one complex structure.
The $i$ of Theorem~\ref{thm:equiv} is supplied by the harmonic dynamics that Eq.~\eqref{eq:central} already presupposes, rather than imported from Dirac theory.

\section{The full-angle variables and the geometric origin of the quartic energy}
\label{sec:quartic}

We now turn to Puzzle~2, the fourth-power character of the energy, which is the central new result of this paper.
The strategy is to change from the half-angle amplitude $\chi$ to the natural \emph{full-angle} observable of a two-level system, its Bloch (Stokes) vector, and to ask what the energies look like there.

\subsection{The Bloch vector as the full-angle observable}

For any normalized two-level amplitude $\chi$, the Bloch (Stokes) vector is the set of Pauli expectation values
\begin{equation}
\Bloch \equiv \chi^\dagger\bm{\sigma}\chi=(\expval{\sigma_x},\expval{\sigma_y},\expval{\sigma_z}),\qquad \abs{\Bloch}=1 ,
\label{eq:blochdef}
\end{equation}
which for the rotor~\eqref{eq:rotor} evaluates, using Eq.~\eqref{eq:pauli}, to
\begin{equation}
\Bloch(t)=(\sin\omega t,\ 0,\ \cos\omega t).
\label{eq:blochval}
\end{equation}
Two points are essential.
First, $\Bloch$ is \emph{bilinear} in $\chi$---it is the same quadratic-in-amplitude degree as a Dirac energy density---whereas $\chi$ itself is the half-angle spinor.
Second, $\Bloch$ rotates at the \emph{full} angle $\omega t$ (a great circle in the $x$--$z$ plane, period $2\pi/\omega$), while $\chi$ rotates at $\omega t/2$ (period $4\pi/\omega$).
The map $\chi\mapsto\Bloch$ is thus two-to-one---it is the Hopf map, the double cover $SU(2)\to SO(3)$---and it is the single change of variables around which this section turns.

\subsection{The energy channels are quadratic forms on the Bloch sphere}

Rewriting the energies~\eqref{eq:channels} in terms of $\Bloch$ is elementary.
Using $\cos^2(\omega t/2)=(1+\cos\omega t)/2=(1+s_z)/2$ and $\sin^2(\omega t/2)=(1-s_z)/2$, and $\sin^2\omega t=s_x^2$,
\begin{align}
T_{e1}&=E_0\,n_1^2=E_0\!\left(\frac{1+s_z}{2}\right)^{\!2}=\tfrac14 E_0(1+s_z)^2,
\label{eq:Te1q}\\
T_{e2}&=E_0\,n_2^2=E_0\!\left(\frac{1-s_z}{2}\right)^{\!2}=\tfrac14 E_0(1-s_z)^2,
\label{eq:Te2q}\\
\gamma^*_{K\!E}&=\tfrac12 E_0\sin^2\omega t=\tfrac12 E_0\,s_x^2 .
\label{eq:KEq}
\end{align}
Every channel is now a \emph{quadratic form} in the components of the full-angle Bloch vector.
Their sum reproduces the conservation law directly, using $s_x^2+s_z^2=\abs{\Bloch}^2=1$:
\begin{equation}
\frac{T_{e1}+T_{e2}+\gamma^*_{K\!E}}{E_0}=\tfrac12(1+s_z^2)+\tfrac12 s_x^2=\tfrac12(1+\abs{\Bloch}^2)=1 .
\label{eq:conserv}
\end{equation}
The fourth-power appearance of Eq.~\eqref{eq:channels} is therefore \emph{entirely} an artifact of expressing the energies in the half-angle amplitude $\chi$; in the full-angle variable $\Bloch$ the same energies are quadratic, of the bilinear degree standard for a Dirac energy density.
The quartic and the quadratic descriptions are one structure pulled back through the double cover:
\begin{equation}
\underbrace{\text{quartic in }\chi}_{\text{spinor / half-angle}}
\;=\;
\underbrace{\text{quadratic in }\Bloch}_{\text{Bloch / full-angle}}
\;\circ\;
\underbrace{(\chi\mapsto\Bloch)}_{\text{Hopf double cover}} .
\label{eq:pullback}
\end{equation}

\subsection{The quartic is derived, not postulated}

Equations~\eqref{eq:Te1q}--\eqref{eq:KEq} do more than re-express the energies: they show the quartic form is \emph{forced} by the model's own relations, so that no independent Stefan--Boltzmann postulate is needed.
The inputs are exactly three, and all three are already part of the model.

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em,itemsep=0.25em]
\item[(i)] \textbf{The radiation-gradient relation}~\cite{ref:p1}, $\partial_\theta(T_{e2}-T_{e1})=E_0\sin\theta$.
Integrating with $T_{e1}(0)=E_0,\ T_{e2}(0)=0$ gives
\begin{equation}
T_{e2}-T_{e1}=-E_0\cos\omega t=-E_0\,s_z .
\label{eq:diff}
\end{equation}
\item[(ii)] \textbf{The oscillator kinetic energy}~\cite{ref:p10}, i.e.\ the simple-harmonic assumption, which makes the captured-photon kinetic energy the quadratic-in-coherence oscillator energy,
\begin{equation}
\gamma^*_{K\!E}=\tfrac12 E_0\sin^2\omega t=\tfrac12 E_0\,s_x^2 .
\label{eq:ke}
\end{equation}
\item[(iii)] \textbf{Energy conservation}, $T_{e1}+T_{e2}=E_0-\gamma^*_{K\!E}=E_0\big(1-\tfrac12 s_x^2\big)=\tfrac12 E_0\big(1+s_z^2\big)$, where the last step uses $s_x^2=1-s_z^2$.
\end{enumerate}

Solving the two linear equations for $T_{e1}$ and $T_{e2}$,
\begin{align}
T_{e1}&=\tfrac12\big[(T_{e1}+T_{e2})-(T_{e2}-T_{e1})\big]
\notag\\
&=\tfrac12\big[\tfrac12 E_0(1+s_z^2)+E_0 s_z\big]
=\tfrac14 E_0(1+s_z)^2 ,
\label{eq:derive}
\end{align}
and likewise $T_{e2}=\tfrac14 E_0(1-s_z)^2$.
Since $\tfrac14(1+\cos\omega t)^2=\cos^4(\omega t/2)$, this is exactly Eq.~\eqref{eq:channels}.
We record the result as a proposition.

\begin{proposition}[Geometric origin of the quartic]
\label{prop:quartic}
Given the radiation-gradient relation~\eqref{eq:diff}, the oscillator kinetic energy~\eqref{eq:ke}, and energy conservation, the thermal energies are uniquely $T_{e1,e2}=\tfrac14 E_0(1\pm s_z)^2$, quadratic forms on the Bloch sphere whose pullback through the double cover $\chi\mapsto\Bloch$ is the quartic $E_0\cos^4(\omega t/2),\ E_0\sin^4(\omega t/2)$.
No independent Stefan--Boltzmann assumption is used.
\end{proposition}

\subsection{Resolution of the root question}
\label{sec:root}

We can now address the root question of Sec.~\ref{sec:intro}: is the electron's fermionic fourth power thermodynamic or geometric in origin?

Proposition~\ref{prop:quartic} shows that the fourth power is \emph{not} an independent physical input.
It is the double-cover pullback~\eqref{eq:pullback} of a quadratic energy on the Bloch sphere, and that quadratic energy is fixed by conservation together with two relations---\eqref{eq:diff} and~\eqref{eq:ke}---that the model already assumes for other reasons.
The Stefan--Boltzmann $T^4$ reading remains \emph{consistent}: identifying a kernel temperature $T_i\propto\cos(\omega t/2)$ or $\sin(\omega t/2)$ reproduces the same $\cos^4,\sin^4$.
But it is no longer \emph{primitive}: the $T^4$ scaling is a re-description of the quadratic Bloch structure, not a second, independent origin.

The dichotomy therefore dissolves into a duality, and one relation is its hinge.
The load-bearing input~\eqref{eq:diff}, $T_{e2}-T_{e1}=-E_0 s_z$, reads simultaneously as a \emph{thermodynamic} statement (the temperature imbalance of the two black bodies) and as a purely \emph{geometric/kinematic} one (the population inversion $s_z=\expval{\sigma_z}$ of the spinor rotation).
These are the same function of time in two languages.
Our conclusion is thus not that thermodynamics is wrong but that it is not fundamental \emph{here}: the fermionic fourth power has a geometric origin---it is the signature of the $SU(2)\to SO(3)$ double cover acting on a standard quadratic energy---and the thermodynamic $T^4$ is the dual reading of that same geometry.
In the language of Puzzle~2: the 0-Sphere model does not contradict the bilinear structure of Dirac theory; it embeds it, with the apparent quartic being the bilinear seen through the spinor half-angle.

This also answers, in the affirmative, an open question of the hyperspherical square-root paper~\cite{ref:p64}.
Its Appendix~C notes that the square-root signature of the energy identity parallels Dirac's factorization and asks whether the parallel is ``more than formal.''
It is: Proposition~\ref{prop:quartic} shows the quartic is the double-cover pullback of a quadratic Bloch energy, \emph{dynamically} realized by the first-order evolution~\eqref{eq:foe} and the symplectic complex structure of Sec.~\ref{sec:symplectic}, with the quartic derived rather than posited.
The parallel is therefore substantive rather than formal.
We add a consistency remark: the rotor is, at each instant, a definite point on the Bloch sphere sweeping a great circle, so its equal $\pm$energy superposition is a coherent, deterministic rotation, not a probabilistic mixture; this matches the time-sequential, non-probabilistic reading of the two sheets adopted in Ref.~\cite{ref:p64}, and does not reinstate the simultaneous four-state spin reading that Ref.~\cite{ref:p64} sets aside.

\subsection{Fermion versus boson, unified}
\label{sec:fermionboson}

The same variable change explains why the fourth power is specific to the electron and absent for the photon.
The bosonic (photon) identity of the 0-Sphere model~\cite{ref:p38} is the \emph{quadratic}, full-angle statement $\cos^2\theta+\sin^2\theta=1$, with no half-angle, no double cover, and no cross term.
For the boson, the amplitude is already the full-angle object: its ``Bloch'' description and its amplitude description coincide, so its energy is quadratic in the amplitude with no residue.
For the fermion, the amplitude is the half-angle rotor $\chi$; its energy is quadratic in the full-angle Bloch vector $\Bloch$, but because $\Bloch$ is itself bilinear in $\chi$, the energy is quartic in the amplitude, and the extra cross term $\tfrac12 E_0 s_x^2$ is exactly the oscillator kinetic energy that has no bosonic analogue.
The quartic-versus-quadratic distinction between fermion and boson is therefore \emph{the double cover itself}, expressed at the level of energy, and not an independent thermodynamic fact about the electron.

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limits}

We state the boundaries of the results plainly, because each marks where a stronger claim would be unearned.

\paragraph{Rest frame only.}
Equations~\eqref{eq:foe}--\eqref{eq:coh} and the equivalence of Theorem~\ref{thm:equiv} describe the zero-momentum sector.
The full Dirac equation requires the kinetic term $c\,\bm{\alpha}\!\cdot\!\mathbf{p}$, Lorentz covariance, and the Clifford algebra $\{\gamma^\mu,\gamma^\nu\}=2\eta^{\mu\nu}$, none of which is derived here.
What has been matched is the rest energy, the square-root structure, and the zitterbewegung frequency; the momentum dependence and covariance remain open, and the construction is at present a rest-frame reduction, not a derivation of the Dirac equation from internal geometry.

\paragraph{Sign sector, not spin sector.}
The equivalence is a two-dimensional embedding into the $\pm$energy (sign) subspace at fixed spin.
The second doubling of the Dirac four-spinor---the spin-up/spin-down $SU(2)$---is not contained in Eq.~\eqref{eq:central} and is carried elsewhere in the model~\cite{ref:p10,ref:p43}.
``Equivalent to the $\pm$ Dirac solutions'' is a two-of-four correspondence.

\paragraph{The root question is settled up to a shared input.}
Proposition~\ref{prop:quartic} removes the fourth power as an independent postulate, but the derivation rests on the radiation-gradient relation~\eqref{eq:diff}.
We have argued that this relation is dual---thermodynamic and geometric at once---so that it does not privilege either origin; a reader who regards Eq.~\eqref{eq:diff} as irreducibly thermodynamic would locate the residual thermodynamic content there.
Our claim is the precise one that the \emph{fourth power itself} is geometric (a double-cover pullback), not that thermodynamics has been eliminated from the model.

\paragraph{Ontology is not settled by a formal isomorphism.}
That both sinusoids are shadows of one rotation is a mathematical identity.
It strengthens the model's realist reading of zitterbewegung but does not establish it: the standard position, that the rest-frame trembling is a representation artifact removed by the Foldy--Wouthuysen transformation~\cite{ref:fw}, remains available, since the physical free electron is a pure positive-energy branch with no trembling.
The present work resolves the origin of the complex structure and of the quartic; it does not, by itself, decide the reality of the oscillation.
The model's falsifiable separation from the standard position stays with the sub-luminal prediction $v_{\text{ZB}}\approx0.04\,c$~\cite{ref:p10}, which contradicts the $\pm c$ eigenvalues of the Dirac velocity operator and is the proper place to adjudicate the ontology experimentally.

\paragraph{A residual factor of two.}
Under Eq.~\eqref{eq:idmass} the two branches carry $\pm mc^2$ and their gap is $\hbar\omega$; if the internal cycle emits one quantum at $\omega$ then $E_0=\hbar\omega=2mc^2$~\cite{ref:p43}, twice the observed rest energy.
Reconciling $E_0=2mc^2$ with the single-electron rest energy $mc^2$---for instance through a binding contribution $E_{\text{bind}}=E_0/2$ at the emission threshold~\cite{ref:p46}---is a bookkeeping task these results surface but do not close.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have shown, at rest, that the amplitude underlying the 0-Sphere model's central identity is a rotor $\chi$ solving a first-order spinor equation whose generator squares to $(\omega/2)^2\openone$; that $\chi$ is unitarily equivalent to an equal superposition of the Dirac positive- and negative-energy rest solutions under $\hbar\omega/2=mc^2$; that the complex unit making this equivalence possible is the symplectic structure of the model's internal harmonic oscillation, with the oscillator's velocity--acceleration pair coinciding component-wise with the Dirac zitterbewegung coherences; and---the central new result---that the fourth-power energy channels are quadratic forms on the Bloch sphere, derivable from the model's own radiation-gradient and oscillator relations together with conservation, and related to the quartic amplitude description by the single Hopf double-cover map.
The threads previously developed apart---the central identity~\cite{ref:p1}, the identification of the kernels with the $\pm$ Dirac branches~\cite{ref:p7}, the harmonic-oscillator kinematics and $\sin2\omega t$ structure~\cite{ref:p10}, the $\theta/2$ spinorial character of the quartic terms~\cite{ref:p43}, and the bosonic quadratic contrast~\cite{ref:p38}---are, at rest, projections of a single phase-space rotation.
In one sentence: one internal oscillator supplies, from one source, the complex structure $i$, the $\pm$ Dirac rest solutions, zitterbewegung, and---through the double cover---the fermionic fourth power.

On the root question we conclude that the electron's fourth power is geometric in origin: it is the $SU(2)\to SO(3)$ double cover acting on a standard quadratic energy, with the Stefan--Boltzmann $T^4$ reading a dual re-description rather than an independent postulate.
This does not eliminate thermodynamics from the model but relocates it: what looked like an irreducible $T^4$ law is the spinor geometry of a two-level system seen in the half-angle variable.
Together with the hyperspherical square-root paper~\cite{ref:p64}, which realizes the same double cover as an elliptic operator on the spatial three-sphere, the present dynamical, complex-analytic treatment supplies the Bloch-vector and first-principles content that the operator-theoretic construction left open, and the two occupy the two ends---Euclidean spatial and Lorentzian temporal---that a full Wick-rotation bridge would join.
The results leave open---and now sharpen---the covariant upgrade beyond the rest frame, the reconciliation of $E_0=2mc^2$ with $mc^2$, and the experimental adjudication of the reality of the internal oscillation through $v_{\text{ZB}}$.
Each is now a well-posed problem rather than a gap.

\section{The Bloch map and the derivation of the quartic}
\label{app:bloch}

This appendix collects the elementary algebra used in Sec.~\ref{sec:quartic} in a self-contained form.

\paragraph{Half-angle to full-angle.}
For the rotor $\chi=(\cos\tfrac{\omega t}{2},\sin\tfrac{\omega t}{2})^{\!\top}$ the occupations are $n_{1,2}=\tfrac12(1\pm\cos\omega t)$, obtained from the half-angle identities $\cos^2(\phi)=\tfrac12(1+\cos2\phi)$, $\sin^2(\phi)=\tfrac12(1-\cos2\phi)$ with $\phi=\omega t/2$.
Hence $s_z=n_1-n_2=\cos\omega t$ and, from $2\cos\phi\sin\phi=\sin2\phi$, $s_x=\sin\omega t$, while $s_y=0$ because $\chi$ is real.
This is the two-to-one map: $\chi$ advances by $\omega t/2$, $\Bloch$ by $\omega t$.

\paragraph{Energies as quadratic forms.}
Substituting $n_1=(1+s_z)/2$ into $T_{e1}=E_0 n_1^2$ gives Eq.~\eqref{eq:Te1q}; likewise $T_{e2}$.
For the cross term, $\sin^2\omega t=s_x^2$ gives Eq.~\eqref{eq:KEq}.
The conservation check~\eqref{eq:conserv} uses only $s_x^2+s_z^2=1$.

\paragraph{Uniqueness of the derivation.}
The linear system in Sec.~\ref{sec:quartic}~C has a unique solution because its two equations---the sum $T_{e1}+T_{e2}$ (fixed by conservation and the oscillator kinetic energy) and the difference $T_{e2}-T_{e1}$ (fixed by the radiation gradient)---determine $T_{e1}$ and $T_{e2}$ separately.
Equation~\eqref{eq:derive} is the explicit solution, and the coefficient structure $\tfrac14(1\pm s_z)^2$ shows the fourth power in $\chi$ is the square of the linear-in-$s_z$ occupation, i.e.\ the double-cover pullback of a quadratic Bloch form.
The Stefan--Boltzmann reading assigns $T_i\propto\{\cos,\sin\}(\omega t/2)$ and recovers the same expressions as $E_0 T_i^4$; the two are numerically identical, confirming they are dual descriptions.

\section{The bosonic contrast}
\label{app:boson}

For completeness we contrast the bosonic (photon) case of the 0-Sphere model~\cite{ref:p38}.
There the normalization identity is the quadratic, full-angle statement
\begin{equation}
\cos^2\theta+\sin^2\theta=1 ,
\end{equation}
with $\theta=\omega t$ and no half-angle.
The photon amplitude is $(\cos\theta,\sin\theta)$ at the \emph{full} angle, so its own ``Bloch'' description coincides with its amplitude description; there is no double cover to pull an energy back through, and the energy is quadratic in the amplitude with no cross term.
Squaring this identity at half angle,
\begin{equation}
\big[\cos^2\tfrac{\theta}{2}+\sin^2\tfrac{\theta}{2}\big]^2
=\cos^4\tfrac{\theta}{2}+\sin^4\tfrac{\theta}{2}+2\sin^2\tfrac{\theta}{2}\cos^2\tfrac{\theta}{2}
=1 ,
\end{equation}
and using $2\sin^2(\theta/2)\cos^2(\theta/2)=\tfrac12\sin^2\theta$, reproduces the fermionic identity~\eqref{eq:central}.
This is the algebraic statement of Sec.~\ref{sec:fermionboson}: the fermion identity is the boson identity squared at half angle, so the fourth power and the extra (zitterbewegung) cross term are precisely what the double cover adds.
