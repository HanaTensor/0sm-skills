I. Thesis — Three Results, One Triangle

The Three Independent Results (Until Now)
Paper #48 demonstrated that the U(1) Berry holonomy yields \(g_{\text{CM}} = 2\) as a topological theorem. Paper #51 identified the phase accumulation functional with a generalized Synge world function and derived photon-sphere confinement to a compact domain via the Bonnet–Myers theorem. These three results have been treated, until now, as logically independent.
The Decisive Observation
The three results are not independent. They are three aspects of the same internal phase accumulation structure. Their simultaneous consistency imposes mathematical constraints that relate the Berry curvature to the Riemann tensor of the emergent metric. The triangle closes when these constraints are jointly satisfied; closure unifies the gauge and gravitational sectors from a single internal geometric object.

The rank-2 symmetric polarization tensor of Paper #58, \(h_{\mu\nu} \propto \varepsilon_{1\mu}\varepsilon_{2\nu} + \varepsilon_{1\nu}\varepsilon_{2\mu}\), belongs to the Lorentz representation \((1,0)\otimes(1,0)|_{\text{sym}} \cong (2,0)\oplus(0,0)\). For this to be a geometrically consistent spin-2 mediator, the Berry connection that generates it must produce a curvature in the corresponding rank-2 Lorentz representation.

The theorem's two key hypotheses (fixed Minkowski background; asymptotic Fock-space states) are simultaneously violated by the closed-triangle construction, providing a structural explanation for why a composite spin-2 mediator is permissible in the 0-Sphere framework.
