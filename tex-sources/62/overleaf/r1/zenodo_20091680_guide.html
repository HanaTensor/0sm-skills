I. Thesis — Two Questions of One Equation

The Bridge and Its Two Debts
The bridge equation \(\gamma = 1 + \lvert a\rvert\) (mass-determining) and its variant \(\gamma_v = 1 + \lvert a\rvert/\sqrt{2}\) (velocity-determining) convert the measured anomalous magnetic moment into an internal Zitterbewegung scale. Two questions have remained open since Paper #10 (2023): what kind of quantity the input \(a\) is, and why the transformation carries the specific factor \(1/\sqrt{2}\) — previously justified only by an alternating-voltage (RMS) analogy.
The Two Results
Input: the anomalous moment is one frame-independent invariant with two representations — an external time ratio \(a_e = T_c/T_a\) (Penning trap) and an internal length ratio \(a_e = (L_0-L)/L_0\) (rotational Lorentz contraction) — joined by the translator \(\gamma_L = 1 + a_e\). Transformation: the factor \(1/\sqrt{2}\) is contained in the conservation identity itself, as the square root of the average-to-peak ratio of the momentum term \(\tfrac{1}{2}\sin^2\theta\). The AC analogy asserted a correspondence; the identity derives a value.

  • Closed-loop reading is an apparent loop observable: assembled from open-path residuals under a closure constraint
Internal Representation (0-Sphere Model)
  • Length ratio \(a_e = (L_0 - L)/L_0\): rotational Lorentz contraction of the internal orbital arc
  • Internally defined geometric quantity (Papers #10, #47)
  • Field-independent by construction; the photon-sphere process is fundamentally an open path \(A \to B \to A'\)

Origin of the Modulus in γ = 1 + |a|
Reversing the internal circulation reverses the velocity vector but leaves \(v_{\mathrm{ZB}}^2\), and therefore the contraction \(L = L_0\sqrt{1 - v_{\mathrm{ZB}}^2/c^2}\), unchanged. The length-ratio representation carries no sign; the modulus is a geometric consequence, not a convention. An external negative anomaly (e.g. the neutron) is read as the same non-negative arc deficit, the sign belonging to the magnetic-response orientation.
The Claim Boundary, Stated with Discipline
Field-independence of \(a_e\) cannot select between descriptions: standard QED computes \(a_e\) as a field-free vertex correction, so field-independence is equally a property of the standard account. The defensible statement adopted throughout: the internal representation is consistent with the field-independent character of \(a_e\) — not forced by it. The section establishes a representation duality, not a privileged representation.

III. The Origin of \(1/\sqrt{2}\) — From Analogy to Derivation

Conservation Identity = Normalized Dispersion Relation
\[ \underbrace{\cos^4\!\tfrac{\theta}{2} + \sin^4\!\tfrac{\theta}{2}}_{(mc^2/E)^2} + \underbrace{\tfrac{1}{2}\sin^2\theta}_{(pc/E)^2} = 1 \]
The mass term and the momentum term \(\gamma^{*}_{\mathrm{KE}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\sin^2\theta\) sum to unity at every phase, exactly as \((mc^2/E)^2 + (pc/E)^2 = 1\). The velocity enters only as the derived square-root quantity \(v/c = pc/E = \sqrt{\gamma^{*}_{\mathrm{KE}}}\).
Central Result: The RMS Ratio Is Identity-Intrinsic
\[ \sqrt{\frac{\langle \gamma^{*}_{\mathrm{KE}} \rangle}{\max_\theta \gamma^{*}_{\mathrm{KE}}}} = \sqrt{\frac{1/4}{1/2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \]
The momentum term averages to \(1/4\) over a cycle and peaks at \(1/2\). Because the velocity is the square root of this term, its cycle representative stands to its peak in the ratio \(1/\sqrt{2}\) — the RMS, derived rather than assumed.
Candidate Cycle Representative Value Status
RMS (square root of mean of \(\sin^2\theta\)) \(1/\sqrt{2} \approx 0.707\) Selected by the identity: velocity is a square-root quantity built on a \(\sin^2\theta\) term
Simple average of \(\lvert\sin\theta\rvert\) \(2/\pi \approx 0.637\) Excluded: averages the velocity instead of the conserved energy quantity
Naive one-half \(0.5\) Excluded: matches neither the energy-level nor the velocity-level reading
Why the Square Root Forces the RMS
The linearly conserved and partitioned quantities are the energy terms, not the velocity. The cycle representative must be taken at the level of the conserved quadratic quantity (its mean), with the velocity following as its square root normalized to the peak — which is exactly the RMS. The 2023 AC-voltage analogy asserted this; the identity now derives it.

IV. The Pulsating-Radius Helix — Geometric Carrier

PROPERTY 1
Radius Equals Momentum
Setting \(R(\theta) = \tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\lvert\sin\theta\rvert\) gives \(R^2(\theta) = \tfrac{1}{2}\sin^2\theta = \gamma^{*}_{\mathrm{KE}}\) exactly. The transverse extent at each phase is fixed by the momentum fraction carried at that phase — the radius is not a free parameter.
PROPERTY 2
Vanishes on the Kernels
\(R(0) = R(\pi) = 0\): the transverse motion disappears at the kernels, recovering the stationary points where the photon-sphere momentum vanishes (\(p_{\gamma^*} = 0\), Paper #7). The constant-radius helix of Paper #51 cannot reproduce this; the \(\sin\theta\) thermal gradient demands a phase-dependent amplitude.
PROPERTY 3
Maximum Amplitude \(1/\sqrt{2}\)
\(R(\pi/2) = 1/\sqrt{2}\) — numerically identical to the RMS factor, and not by coincidence: both are square roots of the same momentum term (\(\sqrt{\max \gamma^{*}_{\mathrm{KE}}}\) and \(\sqrt{\langle\gamma^{*}_{\mathrm{KE}}\rangle/\max\gamma^{*}_{\mathrm{KE}}}\)). The helix is the RMS result expressed geometrically.

V. SU(2) Amplitude vs U(1) Energy — Fixing the Winding

Two Phase Scales in One Identity
The kernel amplitudes \(\cos(\theta/2)\), \(\sin(\theta/2)\) have period \(4\pi\) (720°, SU(2), spinorial); their fourth powers — the thermal energies — and the kinetic term \(\tfrac{1}{2}\sin^2\theta\) have period \(2\pi\) (360°, U(1)). One internal phase presents a 720° structure at the amplitude level and a 360° structure at the energy level (Paper #19). The Zitterbewegung velocity is a property of the kinetic term, so its winding number is unity: a doubled winding would mix in the Thomas-precession (amplitude-level) structure, appropriate for spinorial phase accumulation (Paper #29) but not for the kinetic velocity.
Unit-Winding Helix Reproduces the Factor
\[ \sqrt{\frac{\langle \dot{x}^2\rangle}{\langle \lvert\dot{\mathbf{r}}\rvert^2\rangle}} = \sqrt{\frac{1/2}{1}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \]
With \(x(\theta) = \cos\theta\), \(y = R\cos\theta\), \(z = R\sin\theta\) and \(\lvert d\mathbf{r}/d\theta\rvert^2 = \sin^2\theta + \tfrac{1}{2}\), the longitudinal projection carries \(1/\sqrt{2}\) exactly. A doubled winding does not give this clean value.

VI. The Placement of the Factor — An Open Correspondence

Canonical (\(a\)-level) — Paper #10 \(\gamma_v = 1 + \lvert a\rvert/\sqrt{2}\) \(v_{\mathrm{ZB}} \approx 0.0405c\); at leading order \(a \to a/\sqrt{2}\) scales \(v\) by \(2^{-1/4} \approx 0.841\)

Claim Boundary and Candidate Route
What is established: the factor is identity-intrinsic, and the peak-(mass)/RMS-(velocity) asymmetry is structural. What is not established: that the factor belongs at the level of \(a\). A candidate route is noted without commitment — the pulsating radius is linear in \(\lvert\sin\theta\rvert\), so a contraction deficit proportional to the transverse amplitude would carry exactly the profile whose cycle representative reproduces the \(a\)-level placement. Whether this proportionality follows from the rotational-contraction geometry is part of the open task. Two further internal ratios (the 3:1 time-averaged partition; a \(c/2\) longitudinal representative) were examined and set aside as lacking confirmed observables.

VII. Principal Conclusions

  • Representation duality of the input. The anomalous moment is a single frame-independent invariant with an external time-ratio representation \(a_e = T_c/T_a\) (per-revolution phase slip) and an internal length-ratio representation \(a_e = (L_0-L)/L_0\) (rotational Lorentz contraction), joined by the translator \(\gamma_L = 1 + a_e\) in precise analogy with a single Lorentz boost joining time dilation and length contraction.
  • Geometric origin of the modulus. The length-ratio representation is intrinsically non-negative — the contraction deficit depends only on \(v_{\mathrm{ZB}}^2\), independent of rotation sense — making the modulus in \(\gamma = 1 + \lvert a\rvert\) a geometric consequence rather than a convention.
  • The factor \(1/\sqrt{2}\) is identity-intrinsic. The momentum term \(\tfrac{1}{2}\sin^2\theta\) has time average \(1/4\) and peak \(1/2\); since the velocity is the square root of this term, its cycle representative is necessarily the RMS, with ratio exactly \(1/\sqrt{2}\). The alternating-voltage analogy of Paper #10 is replaced by a derivation.
  • The pulsating-radius helix is the geometric carrier. \(R(\theta) = \tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\lvert\sin\theta\rvert\) gives \(R^2 = \gamma^{*}_{\mathrm{KE}}\) exactly, vanishes on the kernels (recovering the stationary points), and peaks at \(1/\sqrt{2}\) — the same number, from the same term.
  • Winding number fixed to unity. The SU(2) (720°) amplitude versus U(1) (360°) energy duality assigns the velocity description to the energy level; the unit-winding helical projection then reproduces \(1/\sqrt{2}\) exactly.
  • Mass-peak and velocity-RMS on a common footing. The mass is read at the stationary (peak) configuration where the momentum term vanishes; the velocity is read as the cycle representative (RMS) of the momentum term's square root. The asymmetry between \(\gamma = 1+\lvert a\rvert\) and \(\gamma_v = 1+\lvert a\rvert/\sqrt{2}\) is structural, not ad hoc.
  • The placement correspondence is an open task. The derived ratio is velocity-level; the canonical form places the factor at the \(a\)-level; the nonlinear bridge makes them differ by a residual \(2^{1/4}\). The reconciliation — with a candidate route through amplitude-proportional contraction — is recorded, not asserted.
  • Scope stated explicitly. The bridge equation is a reinterpretation within the special-relativistic scope of the Dirac equation: a re-grounding of the Zitterbewegung structure, not a new empirical prediction; gravitational extension lies outside the present paper.
