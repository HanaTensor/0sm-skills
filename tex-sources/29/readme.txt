ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The thermal geodesic was originally introduced in the 0-Sphere model as a
variational principle governing energy transfer between localized kernels,
formulated in terms of particle-wise proper time rather than absolute
coordinate time.

In homogeneous regions, this geodesic reduces to a straight trajectory in real
space, raising the question of how nontrivial internal dynamics arise along
such a simple path. This work shows that relativistic kinematics naturally
embed a spinorial connection along a single thermal geodesic through Thomas
precession. Although the external bosonic motion corresponds to a half rotation
for one-way propagation, the line integral of the spinorial connection along
proper time yields a full 2pi accumulation of internal phase.

The key result is that the relevant geometric quantity is the line integral of
a connection, rather than the curvature of the spatial path. A geometric
correspondence with semicircular geodesics in the Poincare upper half-plane is
presented as an intuitive language for this structure.

Key topics include:
- Thermal geodesic as variational principle in proper time
- Spinorial connection generated by Thomas precession along straight geodesic
- Line integral of connection one-form yielding 2pi phase per one-way traversal
- Poincare upper half-plane as geometric prototype for connection-driven phase
- Dimensional independence: effective dynamics confined to geodesic plane
- SU(2) vs SO(3): why 4pi periodicity survives in spinorial representations
- Correspondence: integral_{gamma} omega_hyp <-> (1/2) integral (a x v) dtau

KEY RESULTS
================================================================================

What the model CAN establish:
- Nontrivial spinorial phase accumulation (2pi) along a straight thermal
  geodesic, arising purely from relativistic kinematics (Thomas precession)
- One-to-one geometric correspondence between Thomas precession connection and
  the hyperbolic connection of the Poincare upper half-plane
- Dimensional independence: spinorial phase accumulation is governed by the
  1D line integral along the geodesic, independent of ambient dimensionality
- SU(2) holonomy is nontrivial even when SO(3) rotation appears trivial
- Framework compatible with established QM and relativistic descriptions

What the model does NOT claim:
- Modification of empirical predictions of QM or GR
- Treatment of specific geodesic equations of motion (deferred to subsequent
  papers; this paper establishes the principled framework only)
