§ 0 Central Thesis
One-Sentence Summary
The electron contains an internal two-kernel architecture whose geometric dynamics — encoded in a single Hamiltonian identity — give rise, without free parameters, to spin, the anomalous magnetic moment, Zitterbewegung, the arrow of time, gravitational radiation, and the spacetime metric.

The 0-Sphere Model is an independent research programme begun in 2018. Its central claim is that the electron is not a point particle but a two-kernel system: two thermodynamic "black-body" kernels (A and B) connected by an oscillating photon sphere. The entire programme is derived from a single Hamiltonian identity — an algebraic energy-conservation constraint — without introducing new fields, new particles, or free parameters.

Two characteristics distinguish this framework from mainstream approaches: (1) it is non-perturbative — the geometry is exact, not an expansion; (2) it employs a directed-path (line-integral) ontology — the primary physical observables are phase accumulations along trajectories, not field values at points.

§ 1 The Foundational Equation — Everything Starts Here

All results in the 0-Sphere programme are derived from a single algebraic identity introduced in Paper #01 (2018). It distributes the total internal energy $E_0$ among three components:

Hamiltonian Identity (Paper #01)
$$E_0 = \underbrace{E_0\cos^4\!\!\left(\frac{\theta}{2}\right)}_{Te_1\;(\text{Kernel A})} + \underbrace{E_0\sin^4\!\!\left(\frac{\theta}{2}\right)}_{Te_2\;(\text{Kernel B})} + \underbrace{\frac{E_0}{2}\sin^2\!\theta}_{E_\mathrm{ph}\;(\text{photon sphere KE})}$$

Here $\theta = \omega\tau$ is the internal phase (proper time), advancing monotonically. The three components satisfy $E_0 \equiv 1$ for all $\theta$ — a topological identity, not a dynamic equilibrium.

AM-GM Floor (Paper #46)
$$Te_1 + Te_2 = E_0\!\left[\cos^4\!\tfrac{\theta}{2} + \sin^4\!\tfrac{\theta}{2}\right] \geq \frac{E_0}{2}$$

The kernel pair always retains at least $E_0/2$ — the geometric zero-point energy. This floor is the origin of $\tfrac{1}{2}\hbar\omega$ in quantum mechanics, derived algebraically rather than from Heisenberg uncertainty.

§ 2 The Framework Boundary — The Central Conceptual Choice

The entire series is built on a single prior choice: which quantities carry primary physical information? Two mutually exclusive answers define two frameworks. The 0-Sphere programme belongs firmly to the directed-path framework.

Primary quantity: field value $\phi(x)$ at spacetime point $x$

Observables: functions of local field values at specific instants

Global/topological properties: secondary — derived from local values

Empirical Anchor: The Aharonov-Bohm Effect
Inside the solenoid: $\mathbf{B} = 0$ identically.
Yet the electron acquires $\Phi = (e/\hbar)\oint\mathbf{A}\cdot d\mathbf{l} \neq 0$, measurable as interference.
Local-field framework: $\Phi$ is an artifact of the gauge potential — not physically real.
Directed-path framework: $\Phi$ is the primary observable; that $\mathbf{B}=0$ along the path is irrelevant.

Wilson loops (gauge theory) and Berry phases (quantum mechanics) are further instances of the same class of directed-path observables invisible to local descriptions. #31

Historical Analogy: Particle Methods in Fluid Mechanics
Eulerian CFD (field values at fixed grid points) cannot easily capture free surfaces, spray, interfacial discontinuities.
Particle methods (SPH, MPS) follow fluid elements along trajectories — a Lagrangian viewpoint — and make these phenomena accessible.

The 0-Sphere framework stands on the same side as particle methods: histories accumulated along trajectories, not field values at points. #55

§ 7 Open Problems — Current Frontier
Foundational / Mathematical
Rank-2 Tensor Proof for Spin-2 (#48, #49, #58)
The $\pi$-periodic $\Omega_T$ numerically corresponds to spin-2 via $s=2\pi/\pi=2$ and the phase-stable pair forms a rank-2 polarization tensor. A rigorous Lorentz-group decomposition showing that the trace-free symmetric part carries helicity $\pm2$ exclusively has not been completed. The SU(2) holonomy framework of #45 is the proposed geometric setting.
Many-Body Extension of Arrow of Time (#56)
Single-electron chirality protection is established. Whether electromagnetic coupling between electrons preserves individual topological protection, and how bosonic fields (photons, phonons) without definite $\chi$ generate a macroscopic temporal direction, requires analysis.
Directed Arc-Length Question (#45)
Is the spinorial arc-length $L_\mathrm{spin}(A,B)$ a scalar (group structure, invertible) or a directed quantity (semigroup, $L(A,B)+L(B,A)\neq0$)? Only the latter would force geometric torsion. No mechanism currently identified requires this departure from standard Lie group fiber bundles.
Quantitative / Experimental
Newton's Constant from Pair Emission Rate (#58)
Inverting $P_\mathrm{GW} \propto \langle\dddot{Q}_{ij}\dddot{Q}^{ij}\rangle$ to extract $G$ from the 0-Sphere internal electron geometry would constitute the first derivation of Newton's constant from electron structure. Requires: quantitative pair emission cross-section, complete rank-2 proof.
Off-Resonance $Y_\ell^m$ Absorption — Problem A (#57)
Standard QED requires a resonance condition. The 0-Sphere framework reframes this: in the directed-path framework, absorption is trajectory-to-trajectory phase matching, not pointwise coupling — eliminating the resonance condition conceptually. A quantitative description (detuning bandwidth, absorption cross-section) is not yet established.
$\delta a_e \approx 6\times10^{-13}$ at GPS Altitude (#57)
The GR extension $a_e^\mathrm{local}=a_e^\mathrm{flat}(1-2\Phi)$ predicts a measurable variation of the anomalous magnetic moment with gravitational potential. Within a factor of six of current Penning trap sensitivity. Free-electron vs. bound-electron correction unresolved.
Axis-Locking Timescale (#59)
Proposition 3.1 states locking occurs within a small number of ZB cycles; the exact number requires solving $d\hat{e}/d\tau=f(\theta,\hat{e},\delta E_\mathrm{Coulomb})$ with an explicit Coulomb potential — the same open task as #52 item (I).
