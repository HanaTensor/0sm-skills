ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The electron is conventionally modeled as a point particle, with internal
structure considered inaccessible or irrelevant to observable phenomena. This
work explores an alternative geometric framework in which the electron possesses
a minimal internal structure consisting of two energy kernels separated by the
Compton wavelength. Energy circulates between these kernels through radiative
exchange, giving rise to an internal geometric phase and a spinorial SU(2)
structure. Observable interference patterns emerge from the quartic energy
identity governing this internal circulation.

When internal phases are not externally controlled, ensemble averaging yields a
universal visibility bound of <V> = sqrt(2) - 1 approximately 0.4142 for
electron interference. Application of relativistic kinematics to the internal
oscillatory motion leads to a characteristic velocity v_zb approximately 0.04c,
derived from Lorentz contraction and the experimentally measured anomalous
magnetic moment. This velocity represents a concrete prediction whose
experimental verification or refutation would directly test the physical reality
of internal Zitterbewegung dynamics.

Key topics include:
- Two-kernel internal geometry and quartic energy identity
- SU(2) spinorial structure from first-principles geometry
- Universal visibility bound <V> = sqrt(2) - 1 for electron interference
- Intrinsic visibility reduction independent of decoherence
- Zitterbewegung velocity v_zb approximately 0.04c derived from
  anomalous magnetic moment
- Monte Carlo simulation confirming ensemble-averaged visibility
- Contrast between electron (quartic) and photon (quadratic) interference

KEY RESULTS
================================================================================

What the model CAN derive:
- Universal visibility bound <V> = sqrt(2) - 1 approximately 0.4142
  from the quartic energy identity without free parameters
- Zitterbewegung velocity v_zb approximately 0.04047c from Lorentz
  contraction and experimentally measured anomalous magnetic moment
- SU(2) spinorial structure from two-kernel internal geometry
- Contrast between electron (quartic, intrinsically limited visibility)
  and photon (quadratic, no intrinsic visibility limit) interference

What the model CANNOT currently derive:
- A priori value of the anomalous magnetic moment a_e (used as input)
- Precise quantitative comparison with specific experimental visibility
  data (source geometry and detector characteristics required)
- Dynamical formulation of internal polarization-resolved radiative
  structure

The visibility bound and Zitterbewegung velocity are falsifiable predictions.
Failure to observe internal motion at approximately 0.04c in future
high-resolution experiments would place sharp constraints on the framework.
