ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The cosmological constant problem is commonly characterized as a discrepancy
of more than one hundred orders of magnitude between theoretical estimates of
vacuum energy density and observational bounds. This discrepancy is
conventionally formulated within a field-theoretic ontology in which quantum
vacuum fluctuations are attributed physical energy density permeating spacetime.

This work argues that, within the framework of the 0-Sphere model, this problem
is not resolved by dynamical or symmetry-based mechanisms but rather dissolved
at the level of principle. The key observation is that the enormous vacuum
energy arises only after adopting local quantum fields as ontologically
fundamental entities. By contrast, the 0-Sphere model takes proper-time
parametrized worldline processes as primary, while spacetime and local fields
appear only as effective descriptions. As a consequence, vacuum energy does not
arise as a gravitational source, and the usual accumulation of zero-point
energies never becomes a well-defined physical quantity.

Key topics include:
- Integral-based ontology as alternative to field-theoretic vacuum energy
- Proper-time parametrized worldline processes as fundamental entities
- Phase accumulation Phi = integral_{gamma_int} omega along internal worldlines
- Vacuum energy as absent gravitational source in 0-Sphere model
- Dissolution vs resolution: ontological reorganization without fine-tuning
- Cosmological constant as open question (boundary conditions, new physics)
- Falsifiability: direct gravitational effects from vacuum fluctuations

KEY RESULTS
================================================================================

What the model CAN derive:
- Dissolution of the vacuum energy problem at the level of ontological principle
- Physical observables restricted to integral quantities (no local mode sums)
- Demonstration that zero-point energy never arises as a well-defined quantity
- Identification of the three assumptions driving the cosmological constant
  problem: pre-existing spacetime background, physical zero-point energies,
  gravitational coupling of vacuum energy
- Clear empirical falsifiability criterion

What the model CANNOT currently derive:
- Numerical value of the observed cosmological constant Lambda_obs
- Explanation of why Lambda_obs is small but nonzero
- Mechanism for boundary conditions, collective effects, or new physics
  underlying Lambda_obs

The dissolution is conceptual: the problem does not arise within the framework,
rather than being suppressed or cancelled by a dynamical mechanism.
