I. Thesis — Inversion of Ontological Priority

Conventional Electrodynamics
The field strength \(F_{\mu\nu}\) (equivalently \(\mathbf{E}\), \(\mathbf{B}\)) is taken as the primary physical object, while the vector potential \(A_\mu\) is treated as an auxiliary quantity burdened with gauge redundancy. The Aharonov–Bohm effect elevates the closed-loop holonomy \(\exp(ie\oint A_\mu\,dx^\mu/\hbar)\) to the status of true observable, yet still privileges the closed loop over the connection itself.
0-Sphere Reformulation
The physically primary quantity is neither the field strength nor a closed-loop holonomy. It is the open-path line integral of an internal 1-form \(\Pi\) along an actual thermal geodesic. The field strength \(F = d\Pi\) emerges as derived curvature, and all four Maxwell equations appear either as mathematical identities or as stationarity conditions of an action functional.

II. Physical Postulate — The Open Wilson Line

Central Observable
\[ W[A \to A'] \;\equiv\; \int_{\gamma:\,\mathbf{r}_A}^{\mathbf{r}_{A'}} \Pi \]
The phase accumulated by the photon sphere along the thermal geodesic \(\gamma\) from the kernel position \(\mathbf{r}_A\) to \(\mathbf{r}_{A'}\). The endpoints are physically determined kernel positions, and the path is always open, \(\mathbf{r}_{A'}\neq\mathbf{r}_A\).
Radiation Gradient (Driving Force)
\[ \Delta E_{AB} = E_A - E_B = E_0\cos(\omega_{\text{ZB}}\,t) \]
The force propelling the photon sphere along \(A \to B \to A'\). An algebraic consequence of the quartic energy partition \(E_A=E_0\cos^4\theta,\ E_B=E_0\sin^4\theta\) with \(\theta=\omega_{\text{ZB}}t/2\) (Appendix A).
Wilson Line Response (Quadrature)
\[ W(t) = W_0\sin(\omega_{\text{ZB}}\,t) \]
Resonant quadrature response of a driven harmonic oscillator with a \(\pi/2\) phase lag relative to the cosine driving gradient (Appendix B). Phase accumulates orthogonally to the driving force.

Structural Consequences

Openness as Structural Feature
The electron is in motion; the kernel never returns exactly to its point of departure. The openness of the path is intrinsic to the radiation–recondensation cycle, not a contingent approximation. Even when external constraints force oscillatory center-of-mass motion, the internal phase history remains non-retracing.
\(\mathbf{E}\), \(\mathbf{B}\) as Derivatives of \(W\)
The electric field emerges as the temporal derivative: \(\int\mathbf{E}\cdot d\boldsymbol{\ell} = -\,dW/dt \propto -\Delta E_{AB}\). The magnetic field emerges as the transverse variation: \(\partial W/\partial(\delta\Sigma) = B_\perp\). Neither relation requires additional dynamical input.
Closed Loops as Derived Objects
The Aharonov–Bohm phase is reconstructed as the difference of two open paths \(\gamma_1,\gamma_2\) sharing endpoints: \(H = W[\gamma_1]-W[\gamma_2] = \oint_{C_{12}}\!\Pi = \Phi_B\). The closed loop is a derived object composed from open paths, never a primary postulate.

IV. Strict Separation of the Two Sectors

Spinorial (Berry) Sector
  • Degree of freedom: kernel spin
  • Path topology: closed (\(\mathbf{r}_{\text{end}}=\mathbf{r}_{\text{start}}\))
  • Periodicity: \(4\pi\) — spinorial, SU(2)
  • Primary quantity: holonomy \(\oint\Pi\)
  • Gauge invariance: full (closed loop)
  • Physical output: spin quantization, anomalous magnetic moment
  • Maxwell role: not applicable
Electromagnetic (Wilson) Sector
  • Degree of freedom: photon-sphere translation
  • Path topology: open (\(\mathbf{r}_{A'}\neq\mathbf{r}_A\))
  • Periodicity: \(2\pi\) — bosonic, U(1)
  • Primary quantity: Wilson line \(\int_A^{A'}\!\Pi\)
  • Gauge invariance: boundary-relative
  • Physical output: electric field, magnetic flux
  • Maxwell role: \(F=d\Pi\), \(dF=0\)
Category Distinction
The two sectors employ different path topologies and yield different physical outputs. Asking whether the electromagnetic Wilson line satisfies a \(2\pi\)-modular Berry condition would be a category error. The open-path structure is not a weakness but a precise reflection of the photon sphere's translational motion between successive kernel encounters.

V. Scale Separation and Experimental Discriminant

Continuum Limit Condition
\[ \ell \;\gg\; 2a \;\sim\; \lambda_{\text{ZB}} \;=\; \frac{2\pi c}{\omega_{\text{ZB}}} \;\approx\; 3.8\times 10^{-10}\ \mathrm{m} \]
When the observation scale \(\ell\) sufficiently exceeds the Compton-scale kernel separation, the \(S^0\) source structure is unresolved, and the discrete Wilson-line structure is interpolated to a smooth 1-form field \(\Pi(\mathbf{r},t)\).
Retarded Interpolation
\[ \Pi(r,t) = \frac{W_0}{r}\sin\!\left[\omega_{\text{ZB}}\!\left(t-\tfrac{r}{c}\right)\right] \]
The natural solution selected by macroscopic causality, boundary data, and smoothness at scales \(\ell\gg\lambda_{\text{ZB}}\). It automatically satisfies \(\Box\Pi = 0\) in the Lorenz gauge (Appendix C). The wave equation emerges as an integrability condition, not as independent dynamics.

Sub-Cycle Experimental Signature

Predicted Discrete Jumps
Maxwell predicts a smooth continuum field; the 0-Sphere model predicts discrete Wilson-line jumps at intervals \(\Delta t \sim \pi/\omega_{\text{ZB}} \approx 6.3\times 10^{-19}\) s.
Concrete Observable
Within each Zitterbewegung cycle, an anticorrelation appears between the photon-sphere occupation \(E_{\text{ph}}\propto\sin^2(\omega_{\text{ZB}}t)\) and the electromotive force \(\mathcal{E}\propto-\cos(\omega_{\text{ZB}}t)\).
Accessibility Horizon
Sub-femtosecond time resolutions approaching \(\pi/\omega_{\text{ZB}}\) remain an active target for attosecond spectroscopy (Krausz & Ivanov, 2009), opening a potential path toward experimental discrimination below \(\lambda_{\text{ZB}}\).

VII. Principal Conclusions

  • Primary quantity. The open-path Wilson line \(W[A\!\to\!A'] = W_0\sin(\omega_{\text{ZB}}t)\) driven by the inter-kernel gradient \(\Delta E_{AB}=E_0\cos(\omega_{\text{ZB}}t)\). Path openness \((\mathbf{r}_{A'}\neq\mathbf{r}_A)\) is a geometric feature of the 0-sphere trajectory, not an approximation.
  • Field strength as curvature. \(F = d\Pi\) is a derived 2-form. Two of the four Maxwell equations follow from \(dF = d^2\Pi = 0\) as mathematical identities.
  • Maxwell as variational limit. The remaining two Maxwell equations arise as stationarity conditions of \(S = -\tfrac{1}{4\mu_0}\int F\wedge\star F\).
  • Maxwell as low-energy limit. At \(\ell\gg\lambda_{\text{ZB}}\) the discrete Wilson-line structure reduces to the smooth continuum of classical electrodynamics. The wave equation \(\Box\Pi = 0\) emerges as an integrability condition rather than an independent axiom.
  • Sector separation. Berry-phase physics (spinorial, closed, \(4\pi\)) and electromagnetic physics (Wilson, open, \(2\pi\)) are structurally distinct sectors of the 0-Sphere framework and must not be conflated.
  • Experimental discriminant. The anticorrelation of \(E_{\text{ph}}\) and \(\mathcal{E}^2\) within each \(\omega_{\text{ZB}}\) cycle provides a potential sub-cycle signature that may distinguish the 0-sphere dynamics from classical Maxwell theory at scales \(\lesssim\lambda_{\text{ZB}}\).
