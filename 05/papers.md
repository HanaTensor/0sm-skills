## Paper #42: On the Non-Perturbative Nature of the 0-Sphere Model and Magnetic Monopole Absence: A Supplement on Structural Analysis and Physical Interpretation

**Date:** 2026-03-04 · **DOI:** 10.5281/zenodo.18857609 · **Type:** Supplement to #36 + #33

### Core Concept

Why magnetic monopoles are structurally absent from the 0-Sphere Model, derived from the asymmetry of the energy conservation identity.

### Energy Identity Asymmetry

```
E₀ = E₀[cos⁴(ωt/2) + sin⁴(ωt/2) + (1/2)sin²(ωt)]
```

- **Single-term** `(1/2)sin²(ωt)`: kinetic energy of the photon sphere — globally coherent, undivided → identified with **electric charge as a monopole quantity** (Gauss's law applies).
- **Paired-term** `cos⁴(ωt/2) + sin⁴(ωt/2)`: potential energies at spatially separated kernels A and B — inseparably coupled → **magnetization as an intrinsically dipolar quantity** (Ampère's law governs).

Because magnetization emerges from the gradient of the paired potential structure, no standalone magnetic monopole degree of freedom appears. The two kernels are dynamically inseparable through the photon sphere; no mechanism exists to isolate a single kernel and assign it an independent magnetic charge.

### Connection to Line-Integral Framework

The structural prohibition is further understood through the open-path vs closed-surface distinction of the line-integral trilogy (#29–#31): the primacy of open-path line integrals is incompatible with a closed-surface Gauss's law for magnetism, while the globally coherent photon sphere yields well-defined monopole charge for electricity.

### Methodological Stance

Conclusions presented as a **working structural hypothesis**, not a completed derivation. A rigorous geometric framework connecting the energy identity to Maxwell-type equations, fiber bundles, and Chern classes remains under development. Follows #36's methodological framework.

### Key Results

- Single-term photon-sphere contribution ↔ electric charge (monopole via Gauss's law)
- Paired-term kernel contribution ↔ magnetization (dipole via Ampère's law)
- Working hypothesis for magnetic dipole inseparability from the architecture of the energy identity
